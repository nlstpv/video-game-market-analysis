# Video Game Market Analysis

## Project Overview

This project explores historical video game sales to identify market trends and factors associated with commercial success. The objective is to provide data-driven insights that can support marketing strategy and product planning for the upcoming year.

The analysis includes exploratory data analysis, regional market segmentation, statistical hypothesis testing, and business recommendations based on historical sales data.

---

## Business Task

A video game retailer wants to improve planning for the next marketing campaign.

Using historical sales data, the goal is to:

* identify the most promising gaming platforms;
* determine the most profitable game genres;
* compare player preferences across different regions;
* evaluate whether user and critic ratings influence sales;
* provide recommendations for future marketing investments.

---

## Dataset

The dataset contains historical information about video games, including:

* release year;
* gaming platform;
* genre;
* regional sales (North America, Europe, Japan, Other);
* critic scores;
* user scores;
* ESRB age rating.

## Key Findings

* Recent platforms demonstrate the strongest commercial potential, while older platforms rapidly lose market share.
* Shooter and Action games consistently generate the highest global sales.
* Gaming preferences differ significantly across North America, Europe, and Japan.
* Critic scores show a stronger relationship with sales than user ratings.
* Platform popularity changes quickly, making recent market trends more informative than long-term historical data.

---

## Business Recommendations

* Focus future marketing campaigns on modern platforms with growing market share.
* Prioritize high-performing genres such as Action and Shooter for Western markets.
* Adapt marketing strategy to regional preferences, particularly for the Japanese market.
* Consider critic reviews as an additional indicator when evaluating the commercial potential of new releases.
